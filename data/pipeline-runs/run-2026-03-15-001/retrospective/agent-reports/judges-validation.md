## Self-Report: Idea Judges (professor-ludus, dr-loop, devil, cash, scout)
**Phase**: Idea Validation
**Task**: Evaluate 10 brain/puzzle game ideas across 5 criteria
**Output**: 2 PASS (72+), 1 TOP-3 selection, 6 FAIL, 1 VETO

### What Went Well
- **Devil** caught 3 critical design flaws: Signal Lock (exponential overflow), Glitch Escape (fixed-solution exploit), Liar's Manual (statistically unloseable). All 3 were correct kills.
- **Triple-Track system** worked well for brain games — Bureaucrat Panic scored highest on Brain Track, Suspect Sudoku on Reflex Track
- **dr-loop** correctly identified Sequence Lock's flow-state potential (77) despite low depth
- Score spread was wide (34-88) showing genuine discrimination

### What Went Poorly
- Only 2/10 passed the 72 threshold — had to use Top-3 selection for 3rd game
- **Ludus** scored Sequence Lock at 38 — overly harsh for a cognitive speed game (the game shipped fine)
- **Scout** scored Sequence Lock at 39 — "most saturated mechanic" was unfair given the cyberpunk differentiation
- **Cash** scored Debt Spiral at 30 — too focused on IAP potential, ignoring ad-based revenue
- Judge calibration is inconsistent: some judges score 30-90 range, others 50-80

### Lessons Learned
- Brain games naturally score lower on Loop/Scout — these judges are calibrated for reflex games
- The 72 threshold may be too high when all 10 ideas are brain games (genre penalty)
- Devil's veto power (< 40 auto-fail) is extremely effective — keep it
- Cash judge undervalues niche/novel monetization approaches

### Suggestions for Pipeline
- Consider genre-adjusted thresholds: 72 for reflex, 68 for brain, 65 for creative
- Ludus should give more credit to cognitive speed games (not just strategic depth)
- Cash should evaluate ad revenue potential, not just IAP
- Add a "genre bonus" of +3 points when the user explicitly requests a genre
